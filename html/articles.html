Understanding Kernel Methods in Machine Learning

By Jonas | October 15, 2025

Kernel methods are a cornerstone of many powerful algorithms, including support vector machines and Gaussian processes. They allow us to model complex, nonlinear relationships by implicitly mapping data into high-dimensional feature spaces.

In this article, we’ll explore what kernels are, why they’re useful, and how to choose one for your specific problem.

What is a Kernel?

A kernel is a function that computes the similarity between two data points in a (possibly infinite-dimensional) feature space without ever explicitly computing the transformation.

Common Kernels

  • Linear Kernel: \( K(x, y) = x^\top y \)
  • Polynomial Kernel: \( K(x, y) = (x^\top y + c)^d \)
  • RBF Kernel: \( K(x, y) = \exp(-\gamma \|x - y\|^2) \)

Each kernel captures a different notion of similarity. Choosing the right one can significantly affect model performance.
